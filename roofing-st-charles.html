A river-corridor town with real storm exposure

St. Charles is an established Fox River community that spans two counties, with everything from historic homes near the downtown to newer neighborhoods spreading east and west. What ties them together is exposure: open river-valley terrain and the storm tracks that follow it mean hail and high winds aren't a once-a-decade event here. Over time, that weather quietly wears a roof down — lifted shingles, bruised granules, and flashing that gives up before the rest of the roof does.

We're local — headquartered in Batavia, not parachuting in behind a storm — so we know what these roofs go through season after season. We give a straight assessment of what's storm-related and what's simple age, and we don't recommend a replacement you don't need.
